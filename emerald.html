Our Products

Utensil Detergent

When cleaning utensils, it is best to use an all-purpose dish detergent. Be sure to use a gentle detergent, as harsher detergents can damage the utensils. It should be gentle on your utensils and doesn't leave a soapy residue.

Bathroom Detergent

Our detergent is used to sed to remove dirt, soap scum, and other debris from surfaces in the bathroom. designed for use on many different surfaces, including tile, porcelain, and acrylic. Some common ingredients found in our bathroom detergents are surfactants, fragrances, enzymes, and bleach. Surfactants work by breaking down dirt and oils so they can be washed away. Fragrances are added to make the detergent smell good. Enzymes work to break down specific types of stains and odors. Bleach acts as a disinfectant and helps to brighten whites. When used as directed, bathroom detergent can help keep surfaces clean and free of germs and dirt.

Toilet Detergent

Our toilet detergent is a type of cleaning product designed specifically to clean and remove dirt and debris from the inside and outside of a toilet. It usually comes in the form of a liquid and maybe scented or unscented. It can be poured into the toilet bowl or spread onto the surface of the toilet with a scrub brush and can also leave a pleasant scent.
