Our Products

Utensil Detergent

Our Utensils detergent is a cleaning solution specifically designed to clean utensils such as plates, bowls, glasses, and cutlery. The Utensils detergent is often formulated to effectively remove food residue, grease, and stains from the surface of utensils, leaving them clean and hygienic. It can be used in both manual and dishwasher cleaning. When cleaning utensils, it is best to use an all-purpose dish detergent. Be sure to use a gentle detergent, as harsher detergents can damage the utensils. It should be gentle on your utensils and doesn't leave a soapy residue.

Bathroom Detergent

Our detergent is used to remove dirt, soap scum, and other debris from surfaces including tile, porcelain and acrylic in the bathroom. Some common ingredients found in our bathroom detergents are surfactants, fragrances, enzymes, and bleach. Surfactants work by breaking down dirt and oils so they can be washed away. Fragrances are added to make the detergent smell good. Enzymes work to break down specific types of stains and odors. Bleach acts as a disinfectant and helps to brighten whites. When used as directed, bathroom detergent can help keep surfaces clean and free of germs and dirt.

Toilet Detergent

Our toilet detergent is a cleaning solution used to clean toilets and remove stains, dirt, and grime. It is specifically formulated to clean and sanitize the surfaces of toilets, keeping them fresh and hygienic. It usually comes in liquid form and can be scented or unscented. It can be used for manual cleaning or with a toilet cleaning brush. The ingredients in toilet detergents can include enzymes, fragrances, and specialized ingredients to address specific cleaning needs such as removing hard water stains or combating unpleasant odors. It can be poured into the toilet bowl or spread onto the surface with a scrub brush and can also leave a pleasant scent.



How to Use





Utensil Detergent


1. Fill a sink or dishwashing basin with warm water
2. Add the recommended amount of detergent to the water
3. Place the dirty utensils into the water and let them soak for a few minutes
3. Scrub the utensils with a scrub brush or sponge to remove any stubborn food residue or stains
4. Rinse the utensils thoroughly with clean water
5. Dry the utensils with a clean cloth or let them air dry.

Toilet Detergent


1. Pour the desired amount of detergent into the toilet bowl or spread it on the surface with a brush.
2. Scrub the inside of the bowl and the outside surfaces of the toilet with a brush or scrubber.
3. Let the detergent sit for a few minutes, as directed on the product label, to allow it to work on stains and dirt.
4. Flush the toilet to rinse away the detergent and debris.
5. Repeat the process, if necessary, until the toilet is clean and sanitized.
6. Remember to use gloves and follow all safety precautions listed on the product label when using toilet detergents.

Bathroom Detergent


1. Wet the surface you want to clean.
2. Apply a small amount of bathroom detergent to the surface.
3. Scrub the surface with a cloth or brush to remove dirt, soap scum, and other debris.
4. Rinse the surface thoroughly with water.
5. Wipe the surface dry with a clean cloth, exercise caution when using bleach or other cleaning agents to avoid damage to surfaces or skin.



Pre Cautions

Emerald Sparkle should not be mixed with other soaps, detergents or ACIDS!

If the solution is accidentally splashed into the eyes, flush throughly with clean water!
